# Timing And Positioning

Use this note when the project may be strong but acceptance, attention, or impact depends on when and how it is framed.

## Timing questions

- Is the idea too early for the community to appreciate?
- Is the topic still hot enough that the paper will matter by the time it appears?
- Is there a risk someone else will publish the same core idea first?

## Positioning rules

- Explain why the problem matters now, not just in the abstract.
- If the work is early, connect it to something real that already exists today.
- Do not be deceptive about motivation; bridge from present reality to future importance honestly.
- If the field has not accepted the premise yet, the introduction may need to sell the future before it can sell the result.

## Luck and uncertainty

- Timing has an irreducible luck component.
- Rejection can mean "too early" or "the field is not ready", not necessarily "the idea is bad".
- Do not confuse temporary community disinterest with proof that a problem is unimportant.

## Practical outputs

- A short "why now" paragraph for the intro.
- A statement of the intended reader or venue audience.
- A risk note about whether to submit now, pivot audience, or hold until the case is easier to make.
