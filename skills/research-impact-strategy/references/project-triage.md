# Project Triage

Use this note once a project exists and the question becomes whether to keep investing.

## Default posture

- De-risk early by testing the sub-problem most likely to fail.
- Do not sink months into the polished version before a smaller prototype validates the core idea.
- Separate "technically working" from "worth becoming a paper".

## Decision rules

### `GO`

- The core idea matters.
- The current evidence supports the framing.
- The missing work is mostly execution quality, not conceptual rescue.

### `KILL`

- The project does not matter enough even in its best case.
- The idea depends on assumptions that keep failing contact with reality.
- The cleanest truthful conclusion is still weak.

### `PIVOT`

- The work uncovered something interesting, but not the original intended claim.
- A different framing, audience, or problem statement makes the evidence more meaningful.
- The strongest version of the paper is not the version originally imagined.

### `SCOPE-DOWN`

- There may be a good paper here, but only if the work is narrowed to one idea.
- The current version is trying to communicate multiple claims or solve too many reviewer objections at once.
- A smaller paper would let the main lesson land cleanly.

## Must-answer evidence

- What is the first skeptical question a strong reader will ask?
- Which baseline, ablation, confounder, or multi-trial check would answer that question?
- What obvious extension or sanity check would make the paper feel complete rather than unfinished?
- What should remain for future work because it is optional rather than essential?

## Anti-patterns

- Continuing because of sunk cost.
- Keeping a low-impact paper alive because the experiments are almost done.
- Adding more experiments without sharpening the claim they are supposed to support.
