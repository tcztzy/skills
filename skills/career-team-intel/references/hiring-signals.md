# Hiring Signals: Taxonomy, Scoring, Interpretation

Use this to turn messy public hints into a calibrated “how likely is this team hiring now?” assessment.

## Signal taxonomy (single artifact)

### Strong signals (S)
- Official job req exists and is recent (or clearly active).
- Team lead / hiring manager explicitly says “we’re hiring” + role/level/location + how to apply.
- Recruiter post explicitly tied to a req/org/team + clear next step.

### Medium signals (M)
- Lead says “team is growing / looking to add people soon” but no req/link.
- Employee says “my team is hiring” but does not provide req/link and is not a manager.
- Multiple independent team-output artifacts strongly suggest a new initiative *and* you find adjacent open roles.

### Weak signals (W)
- Generic evergreen post (“we’re always hiring”) without specificity.
- Old posts (>90 days) without evidence of a still-open req.
- Aggregator listings without an official source.

### Negative signals (N)
- Credible, explicit statement of hiring freeze / no headcount / team downsizing.
- Role reposts without any actual open req, or recruiter says “no longer hiring”.

## Scoring (simple and robust)

Score each “hiring lead” across 4 axes, then map to confidence.

- Authority (0-3)
  - 3: hiring manager / EM / TL / group lead for the team (explicit)
  - 2: recruiter tied to the org/req (explicit)
  - 1: employee (IC) or adjacent team member
  - 0: aggregator / unclear author

- Recency (0-3)
  - 3: 0-30 days
  - 2: 31-90 days
  - 1: 91-180 days
  - 0: >180 days / no date

- Specificity (0-3)
  - 3: role + level + location + “how to apply” (req link/ID preferred)
  - 2: role + “how to apply” but missing level/location/team
  - 1: vague (“we’re growing”) without role details
  - 0: purely generic branding

- Corroboration (0-3)
  - 3: matches an official req (same org/role/location) or multiple official artifacts
  - 2: two independent professional sources (e.g., lead post + team blog)
  - 1: only one source
  - 0: rumor / repost / unverifiable

Penalties:
- Negative evidence: subtract 1-3 depending on credibility and directness.

Mapping:
- 9-12: High confidence hiring now
- 6-8: Medium confidence (confirm needed)
- 0-5: Low confidence (treat as speculative)

## Interpretation patterns (common messages)

- “We’re hiring for X; apply here / DM me” -> strong intent; ask “which req ID should I apply to?”
- “We’re growing / building a new team” -> medium; look for an official req or ask for it.
- “We filled the role / headcount closed” -> negative; stop investing time.
- “Only internal transfers right now” -> negative for external candidates; consider networking only.

## Verification checklist (before you message someone)

- Does the signal have a date and a stable link?
- Is the author’s role/team explicit (on their profile or the post itself)?
- Is there an official req? If yes, capture req ID and align your message to it.
- If no official req: ask a single, simple question (“Do you already have a req ID/link?”).

## Output format (recommended)

For each candidate team, write a 3-6 line block:
- What’s hiring (role/level/location)
- Evidence (1-3 links, dated)
- Confidence (High/Med/Low + score)
- What to do next (apply link, who to message, what to validate)
