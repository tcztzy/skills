# Primary Sources

Use these sources to ground benchmark design choices.

## Assessment and scoring foundations

1. Standards for Educational and Psychological Testing (AERA, APA, NCME; 2014)
- URL: https://testingstandards.net/
- Use for: validity, reliability, fairness, and score interpretation principles.

2. NBME item-writing guide
- URL: https://www.nbme.org/item-writing-guide
- Use for: practical quality rules for high-quality written assessment items.

3. Haladyna, Downing, Rodriguez (2002), *A Review of Multiple-Choice Item-Writing Guidelines for Classroom Assessment*
- URL: https://doi.org/10.1207/S15326977EA0103_03
- Use for: evidence-backed item writing pitfalls and guideline synthesis.

## Multi-step reasoning benchmark exemplars

1. GSM8K: Cobbe et al. (2021), *Training Verifiers to Solve Math Word Problems*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.14168
- Use for: grade-school multi-step arithmetic reasoning task construction.

2. MATH: Hendrycks et al. (2021), *Measuring Mathematical Problem Solving With the MATH Dataset*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.03874
- Use for: structured solution difficulty and mathematical reasoning coverage.

3. BIG-Bench Hard: Suzgun et al. (2022), *Challenging BIG-Bench Tasks and Whether Chain-of-Thought Can Solve Them*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.09261
- Use for: hard reasoning tasks and stress-testing compositional generalization.

4. GPQA: Rein et al. (2023), *GPQA: A Graduate-Level Google-Proof Q&A Benchmark*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.12022
- Use for: expert-level reasoning with reduced simple web-search shortcutability.

## Agentic capability benchmark exemplars

1. AgentBench: Liu et al. (2023), *Evaluating LLMs as Agents*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.03688
- Use for: broad agent capability coverage across interactive environments.

2. WebArena: Zhou et al. (2023), *A Realistic Web Environment for Building Autonomous Agents*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.13854
- Use for: browser-based long-horizon, tool-using agent tasks.

3. SWE-bench: Jimenez et al. (2023), *Can Language Models Resolve Real-World GitHub Issues?*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.06770
- Use for: software-engineering agent evaluation with verifiable issue resolution.

4. OSWorld: Xie et al. (2024), *Benchmarking Multimodal Agents for Open-Ended Tasks in Real Computer Environments*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.07972
- Use for: desktop-computer agent tasks and end-to-end interaction evaluation.

5. GAIA: Mialon et al. (2023), *GAIA: a benchmark for General AI Assistants*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.12983
- Use for: realistic assistant tasks requiring tool use, retrieval, and reasoning.

## Benchmark lifecycle and reliability

1. Dynabench: Kiela et al. (2021), *Dynabench: Rethinking Benchmarking in NLP*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.14337
- Use for: dynamic benchmark refresh and adversarial data collection.

2. HELM: Liang et al. (2022), *Holistic Evaluation of Language Models*
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.09110
- Use for: multi-metric evaluation framing and transparent reporting dimensions.
